    Getting Started Guide

    Just getting started with PoolParty? The following pages are designed to be the first few articles you read when just starting out using PoolParty (PP)

    • PoolParty terminology: Understanding the language of PoolParty is an important step in being able to deploy clouds. Checkout this page to learn about a few common terms used in PP.
    • Understanding the bootstrap and provisioning process: What happens when I type cloud-start ? What is the difference between cloud-bootstrap , cloud-provision , and cloud-configure ? PP translates these simple commands into a large number of commands that are executed on your nodes. Read this to learn about the bootstrap and provisioning process.
    • Understanding Resources: Resources are the meat and most interesting part of any deployment. This page will help you understand what resources are and how to use them.
    • What is Chef?: Chef is PP's default dependency resolver. Your clouds.rb is translated by PP into Chef recipes. Chef then uses those recipes to provision your instances.
    • Using the cloud and pool binaries: PP installs a number of intuitive binary commands that allow you to 'speak' to your cloud. This page goes over some of the things you can do with those binaries.
    • The PoolParty family of gems: suitcase, butterfly, parenting, dslify; What are all these gems and what do they do? Read on to find out.
